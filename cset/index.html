Why CSET?

Hi! My name is Jerod and I’m a student in the SFS majoring in Science, Technology and International Affairs.

I’m fluent with Data Structures and Algorithms in Python. Recently, I’ve been studying statistical methods in an effort to do data analysis on political economy. One area of particular interest to me is quantitative research into political decision-making. Ultimately, I want to work in a niche of public policy to promote human rights abroad.

This summer, I worked in software development and data science for Measurabl, a SaaS company that helps aggregate, report and act on sustainability data.

Measurabl’s tech stack includes Rails for Auth, Java for BE, Angular for FE, Jenkins for CI/CD, Selenium for QA, and a MySQL DB implementation.



In my role, I researched the feasibility of using ML to include waste usage data aggregated from clients into Measurabl’s unique building scoring system. I presented a draft on implementing waste scoring with Gradient Boosting.

Some other projects at Measurabl include designing a workflow to fuzzy match addresses whenever a potential partner provided a list of buildings, and automating tasks from the devops team to help migrate our science workflow to Docker and AWS. Through that, I gained valuable experience debugging data pipelines and implementing hotfixes.
