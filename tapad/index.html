Why Tapad?

2/1/2020

To the Tapad Team:

My name is Jerod and I’m a student at Georgetown University’s School of Foreign Service majoring in Business and Global Affairs. Thanks for posting this internship opportunity!

I enjoy looking for patterns. From AIME problems to Jane Street puzzles, I use opportunities to put analytical concepts into practice.


*I can do some frontend programming.

Last summer, I interned at Measurabl, a SaaS startup that helps companies aggregate, report and act on sustainability data. I worked on the proprietary building scoring system with scikit-learn, created visualizations of our building property types for the sales team and debugged the data pipeline.

During the school year, I work on NLP and data engineering for policy analysis at Georgetown’s Center for Security and Emerging Technology. In my spare time, I’m working on a project analyzing voting patterns in my home state.

So, why Tapad? (why me?)

I want to contribute to a motivated, passionate team that creates value – ideally, on a product that can be reproduced in unlimited quantities.

Tapad’s core beliefs resonate with me.

I’m eager to master anything that’s required and bring a whatever-it-takes attitude to everything I do.

To me, Tapad’s business plan has infinite potential. Accurately connecting customers to relevant products allows consumers to acquire what they need, companies to foster innovation, and makes everybody better off.

I bring a unique skillset with a solid foundation in Computer Science, analytical framework from the Walsh SFS and understanding of business needs from Georgetown’s McDonough School of Business.

For this summer, I believe an internship with Tapad is the right place to start.

Thanks for considering my application!
